00 · Overview

Start here.

If you read one section, read this one. The rest of the document explains the belief, the voice, and the system behind the brand. This page is the company itself, in plain terms: what it is, what it does, who it is for, and why it is different.

What it is

CredShields is a full scope cybersecurity company with deep offensive security expertise, built on more than two decades of hands on work assessing and strengthening security across banking, fintech, healthcare, technology, and other sectors.

What it does

CredShields provides offensive security assessments and validation services to organizations that want to operate securely with confidence. Through hands on, real world testing across web applications, infrastructure, cloud environments, and other critical systems, it finds what scanners miss and proves every finding: reproduced, replayable, and signed by the researcher who found it.

Who it is for

CredShields is built for organizations that need certainty about what’s in their systems. It primarily serves technical CISOs, AppSec leaders, and security teams in banking, fintech, healthcare, and technology sectors who want security assessments grounded in deep offensive security expertise and real world experience.

02 · Positioning

One position no competitor can honestly take.

CredShields competes in the pentest and PTaaS market every buyer already understands. It does not need a new category to win. It needs to own a position no one else can claim. Security someone answers for: proven, authenticated, named.

Who is in the room

Primary · The technical CISO

Owns the security decision and has the depth to judge the work. Feels scanner noise personally. When they believe, the deal happens.

Secondary · The AppSec lead

Closest to the problem, living with scanner noise daily. Becomes the internal advocate the moment you hand them findings credible enough to take to engineering.

Influencer · The board and CFO

Approve the budget and ask who signed off on the assessment. They need a name they can point to. The CVEs and Hall of Fame recognitions do the work here.

The technical CISO, up close

Their frustration

Scanner noise. Findings engineering ignores. Firms that delivered reports and disappeared. And now AI shipping code faster than any team can review what it introduced.

What they want

Certainty, and the accuracy to act on it. Not more coverage, not a longer findings list. The truth about what is actually in their systems, with none of the noise.

What unlocks them

A name on the line. Someone whose reputation rides on the finding alongside theirs.

The most dangerous vulnerabilities are not proven by tools alone. They are proven by people who work like surgeons: further than any tool goes, who refuse to stop until the truth is owned by someone with a name.

The four things that cannot be copied

Inside the real application

We test the real system, past the front door, with real data, real roles, real tenant boundaries. Not staging. Not a black-box scan from the outside.

Across every role and boundary

Where the bugs that matter live: broken cross-role authorization, tenant isolation that leaks, SSO gaps, and business logic that only falls apart when you’re logged in as several users at once.

Proven, not predicted

Not a CVSS score and a theoretical attack path. A reproduced, replayable, authenticated trail your engineers can run themselves, with no false positives to wade through. When you fix it, we verify the fix in seconds.

A named researcher who owns it

Every finding is signed. A named researcher stands behind it, backed by the full team’s depth.

The alternatives, and the gap

The scanner, or the autonomous tool

Fast and cheap at flagging. It cannot reproduce a cross-role exploit, cannot tell you which of a thousand alerts is the one that matters, and leaves you to wade through the false positives.

The crowd, or the platform

Volume and a queue. You get many eyes and no one set of them that knows your system, works the authenticated layer, or takes ownership of what they found.

The traditional pentest firm

A report that lists everything and proves nothing, then disappears until next year. Rarely tests past the front door, rarely returns to verify the fix. The engagement ends where the real work should start.

03 · Personality & Voice

The surgeon.

If CredShields were a person walking into a room, they would be the best surgeon in the room. Not the loudest. The one whose presence changes the quality of every conversation they enter.

Precise

Every word chosen. Nothing wasted. If it can be said in five words, it is not said in ten.

Certain

Does not hedge. States what is true and stands behind it.

Human

Beneath the rigour there is genuine care: for the work, and for the people on the other side of every engagement.

Earned

Does not claim. Demonstrates. The authority comes from the track record, not the declaration.

One voice, two registers

The brand speaks to a CISO and to a protocol founder, and they do not read the same way. The voice flexes to meet them. What it never does is change what it stands for.

What never flexes

The position and the never-say list. Proven, authenticated, named holds on every surface, in every channel, to every audience. The words we refuse stay refused. These are the brand; change them and it stops being CredShields.

What flexes

Register and emphasis. The enterprise surface speaks in the full surgeon’s voice: calm, exact, unhurried. The Web3 surface can move faster and sharper for a founder or developer who lives at that speed. Same position, different tempo.

Where the human shows

Certainty without warmth is just a machine talking. Two places the care has to show, every time.

To the engineer who shipped the bug

Never “you left a critical hole.” Always “this one is easy to miss, here is the exact fix, and here is how to keep it from coming back.” We are on their side, not scoring points.

When we hit the edge of scope

We say so plainly. “We did not get to everything this round. Here is what we would look at next, and why it matters.” Honesty about limits is part of the care, not a crack in it.

Voice in the product

Error and failure states

What happened, why, and the way forward. Never blame, or a bare stack trace, never “Oops, something went wrong.” The same posture as talking to the engineer who shipped the bug: this one is easy to miss, here is the fix.

Scan and log output

Factual and quiet. Severity > specimen > impact, in that order. State what was found, not how hard the tool worked to find it.

API and developer docs

The surgeon’s register: exact, complete, examples that actually run. No marketing voice in reference material. A developer reading the docs should feel the same precision a CISO feels in the proposal.

The tone floor

Across all of it: no exclamation marks, no emoji, no cute empty states or 404s. The product sounds like the person who signs the findings, not like a consumer app.

Logo

Forms

Default to the full lockup, mark plus wordmark. Use the shield mark alone only where the name is already present or space is tight: avatar, favicon, watermark.

Versions

White on deep green, ink, or photography. Deep green or ink on cream and white. Forest green only as a small accent mark. Never the logo on a busy or low-contrast background.

Clear space and size

Keep clear space equal to the height of the shield on every side. Minimum 120px wide for the lockup, 24px for the mark alone. Below that, it breaks.

Never

Do not recolor outside the palette, stretch, condense, rotate, add shadows or gradients, box it in, retype the wordmark in another font, or place white on light or dark on dark.

Co-branding: the audit badge

When CredShields audits a protocol or product, the mark travels onto someone else’s surface. The badge is among the brand’s highest-leverage assets and the easiest to dilute. These rules keep it meaning something.

The mark

The “Audited by CredShields” lockup: the shield plus a short line. Two versions, light for cream and white client surfaces, dark for deep grounds. Same clear space and minimum size as the logo. Never the shield alone: on someone else’s site, the name has to be present.

It is dated and scoped

The badge certifies one engagement at one point in time, not a permanent endorsement. It always carries the date and the scope, and links back to the verifiable report. Code changes; an open-ended badge on changed code is a claim we cannot stand behind.

On the client’s surface

The client’s brand leads; the badge is subordinate but never altered. Same Never list as the logo: no recolor, stretch, shadow, or retype. It always links to the proof. A badge that cannot be verified is the anonymous report the brand exists to oppose.

Imagery

The security category is a swamp of clichés. The fastest way to look like everyone else is to reach for the stock library.

Hooded figures and faceless hackers. Glowing padlocks, shields, keyholes, binary or matrix code rain. Red-on-black breach alarms, skulls, fear imagery. Handshake-over-laptop and blue cyber-tunnel stock.

Real people: actual researchers, real faces, real work. Real artefacts: terminals, code, the dashboard, an actual finding. The brand system: cream and deep green, depth over darkness, lime as a single accent.

The test

Show the work, not a metaphor for the work. If it could sit on any other security vendor’s homepage, it is wrong.

Calm, not alarmed. The surgeon’s room, not the war room. Confidence reads quieter than fear.

The format to own: the post-mortem

The one piece of content that compounds fastest. After a public breach, while everyone else posts hot takes, CredShields publishes the autopsy: what was actually in the authenticated layer, and what the assessment that signed off missed.

How it works

Take a breach the market is still talking about. Reconstruct the failure in the authenticated layer, the class of bug, the exact gap the prior assessment left. Publish it under a researcher’s name, with the proof.

Why it works

It shows the space a risk it has not priced yet: that “assessed” and “secure” are not the same word. The reader does not feel sold to. They feel the gap in their own systems, and they come to close it.

The discipline

AI is the instrument, not the surgeon

AI as tool

AI as threat

The reason the CISO is awake. Autonomous attackers chain low-severity bugs into working exploits faster than any team can triage. This is the enemy the product answers.

“We already run pentests.”

Then you have seen the report that lists everything and proves nothing. The question is whether anyone tested the authenticated layer, and whether a named human will stand behind what they found. That is the gap.

“How are you different from a crowd or a platform?”

Crowds and platforms give you volume and a queue. We give you a named researcher who works the authenticated layer and signs every finding. Proven, not predicted. Owned, not anonymous.

“You are more expensive.”

Compared to a scan, yes. Compared to the breach the scan missed, no. You are paying for proof and a name on the line, not a longer findings list.

08 · Brand Architecture

One house. Everything under it.

CredShields is one brand with products beneath it, not a holding company of separate names. This section sets what a product inherits simply by belonging to CredShields, and what it has to earn on its own. It is the rule that keeps the company from reading as three companies.

The model

A branded house. CredShields leads; products sit beneath and borrow its authority. There is one deliberate exception: SolidityScan arrived with its own Web3 equity, so it is endorsed rather than absorbed, “by CredShields,” not folded into the name. Everything else is master led.

The map

CredShields · the company

The master brand and the full scope security firm. Everything is named under it. Lead with CredShields; products sit beneath and borrow its authority.

CredShields One · the platform

Carries the machine reach: crawling, testing, and re-validating at scale, while the researcher carries the judgment. Master led, speaking entirely in the CredShields voice and inheriting the full position.

SolidityScan · the endorsed brand

The Solidity and EVM scanner, and the Web3 root the company grew from. It kept its own equity, so it is endorsed, not absorbed: the one product that keeps its own name forward.

What inherits, what is earned

Equity and claims

A product inherits the position the moment it ships: proven, authenticated, named, and the proof behind it. It never re-argues them. What it earns on its own is its product proof: the scanner’s accuracy, the platform’s coverage.

Endorsement level

Master led by default. Endorsed only when a product carries its own equity worth keeping. The test is simple: would dropping the CredShields name lose something real? If yes, endorse. If no, lead with CredShields.

Visual inheritance

The logo system, the typefaces, and the green-and-lime palette across its light and dark surfaces carry to every product. A product may have its own interface. It never has its own brand identity.

Verbal inheritance

The position and the never-say list hold everywhere. Register and emphasis flex by surface, as set in Personality and Voice. A product can move at its audience’s speed; it cannot change what the brand stands for.

Writing the names

CredShields, one word, two capitals. “CredShields One” with a space. “SolidityScan” one word, two capitals. Never CredShields1, credshields, or all-caps in body copy.

Governance

  • This document is the single source of truth for the CredShields brand. When anything conflicts with it, this wins.
  • One owner keeps it. Changes route through them, not made ad hoc in a deck or a thread.
  • It is versioned. This is v5. Material changes bump the version and note what changed.
  • Reviewed every quarter, or whenever the product, the market, or the position shifts.

Grammar and ownership

  • Brand name: one word, two capitals, CredShields. All-caps only as a styled wordmark.
  • Sentence case for headings and buttons, not Title Case. Product names keep their casing.
  • No em dashes or spaced dashes. Restructure into two sentences, or use a colon.
  • Numerals for anything measurable: 3 endpoints, 100+ pentests, 40-70% false positives.
  • Oxford comma, always.
  • Findings format: severity, then the specimen, then the impact.
  • Typefaces under the SIL Open Font License: Bricolage Grotesque, Figtree, JetBrains Mono.
  • The logo, wordmark, and this document are CredShields property.
